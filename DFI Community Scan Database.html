Overview

This database shows recent demographic, housing, and economic trends in every county and place in North Carolina.

The data should be consulted as part of DFI's community scan process.

Project team members can download and visualize data as needed.

The database is unable to capture everything that might be important for a project. Project team members should investigate other data sources that they think might be important as part of the community scan process.

Vacancy rate by vacancy type

The Census measures several types of vacancy, including units that are for rent to year-round tenants and units for seasonal or recreational use.

"Other vacant" referes to units that are not inhabited nor on the market for rent or fore sale. Housing researchers generally consider these homes to be vacant due to low-quality or physical neglect and is often used as a measure for housing quality.



Change in rental units by contract rent

This chart shows the number of rental units by contract rent (excluding costs of utilities). The trend shows the overall change in rental units and whether the number of low-cost units (<$350) is increasing or decreashing.

Note: The rent categories for this chart are arbitrary and only meant to capture high-level changes in the rental market. Use the DFI Housing Needs Database to lookup rents are "affordable" to different income brackets.
